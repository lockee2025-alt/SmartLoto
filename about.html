About the Smart LOTO System

Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) is a critical safety control designed to isolate hazardous energy during maintenance and servicing activities, preventing accidental energization of equipment. Traditional LOTO implementations rely heavily on manual procedures, physical locks, and paper-based documentation. While effective when strictly followed, these methods often lack real-time visibility, traceability, and centralized oversight, increasing the risk of human error and unauthorized access.

The Smart LOTO System modernizes this process by incorporating electronic locking mechanisms, biometric authentication, integrated sensors, and centralized digital logging. Access to equipment is restricted exclusively to authorized personnel, while all actions are automatically recorded and auditable. Through integration with the Building Management System (BMS), Smart LOTO provides real-time operational status, enhanced incident response, and improved regulatory complianceâ€”transitioning energy control from manual enforcement to data-driven safety management.

Terms and Policies

1. Purpose

These terms define the appropriate use, responsibilities, and governance of the Smart LOTO System integrated with the Building Management System (BMS). The system is intended to enhance workplace safety, accountability, and visibility during lockout/tagout operations.

2. User Responsibilities

Users must operate the system solely for authorized maintenance and safety activities. All access credentials and biometric data must be safeguarded. Any system irregularities, errors, or suspected unauthorized access must be reported immediately. Tampering or unauthorized modification of hardware or software is strictly prohibited.

3. Administrator Responsibilities

Administrators are responsible for user access management, system configuration, data integrity, and routine inspection of all hardware and software components to ensure continuous and reliable operation.

4. Data Privacy and Security

All collected data, including biometric identifiers and activity logs, is used exclusively for identification, monitoring, and audit purposes. Data processing and storage comply with the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (R.A. 10173) and applicable institutional privacy standards. Appropriate technical and administrative safeguards are implemented to prevent unauthorized access.

5. Hardware and Safety Policy

System hardware may only be installed, handled, or serviced by authorized personnel. Devices must be operated within specified environmental and electrical limits. Power sources must be disconnected prior to installation or maintenance activities.

6. System Connectivity and Availability

Continuous Wi-Fi connectivity is required for real-time monitoring, data transmission, and activity logging. The Smart LOTO System does not support offline operation. Temporary network disruptions may limit access to real-time data and logs.

7. Emergency Override Policy

A mechanical emergency override is provided exclusively for authorized administrators in the event of system failure or biometric authentication issues. All override actions are logged and subject to formal administrative review.

8. Limitations and Liability

The Smart LOTO System is a prototype developed for academic and evaluation purposes. While it has undergone functional and reliability testing, it is not certified for large-scale industrial deployment. The developers and affiliated institution disclaim liability for damages or losses resulting from misuse, unauthorized operation, or improper application of the system.

9. Policy Updates and Compliance

These terms and policies may be revised to reflect system enhancements or regulatory updates. All users and administrators are required to comply with the most current approved version. Continued use of the Smart LOTO System constitutes acceptance of these terms and policies.
